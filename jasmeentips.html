Tips and Management


Strong and Unique password


Cybersecurity experts make the recommendation of unique and strong passwords in order to stay safe. And regularly change passwords.
A strong and unique password provides essential protection from financial fraud and identity thefts.

One of the most common ways that a hacker breaks into a computer is by guessing passwords.
Strong passwords are considerably harder to crack. Which includes:
  • At least seven characters
  • Doesn’t contain a real name, user name or company name.
  • Doesn’t contain a complete dictionary word.
  • Should be significantly different from previous passwords.
  • Should contain characters from these groups: Uppercase letters. Lowercase letters and numerals.



Check your social privacy settings


If you have a social account with a lot of information about you,
you might be surprised that it’s visible to anyone on the internet and
it is a big advantage for a cybercriminal to threat you.

Change your social network account privacy settings.

Here’s how to do it on:
Facebook, Twitter, LinkedIn, Instagram, Snapchat and VK.com.





Keep your software up to date



For your digital protection and cybersecurity, software updates are essential. The main reason for keeping your software updated is to stay protected from security threats.
The older software has the exploitable holes in the code that will allow hackers and cybercriminals to get up to good.

In addition, many of the more malicious malware attacks in common applications such as operating systems and browsers are triggered by software vulnerabilities.

Those are massive systems that need periodic upgrades to maintain their security and reliability. Alternatively, you should interpret these upgrades as a significant stepin protecting your knowledge, rather procrastinating about software updates.



Software updates offer plenty of benefits. Includes:
  • Repairing security holes that have been discovered and fixing or removing computer bugs.
  • Better security- Software updates help protect your data- documents and personal information.
  • You are no longer vulnerable to security issues.




Avoid Phishing scams and beware of suspicious
emails and phone calls.



Scammers use emails, text messages or phone calls to trick you into giving them your personal information.
Here the steps you can use to protect yourself from phishing.

  • Protect your computer and your mobile phone by using security software. Set the software to update automatically so it can deal with any new security threats.

  • Protect your account by using multi-factor authentication.
    Many accounts provide additional protection by allowing two or more login credentials
    which are called multi-factor authentication.

  • Protect your data by backing it up

  • Anti-phishing toolbar- Most popular Internet browsers can be customised with toolbars to prevent phishing.These toolbars easily test the pages you visit
    and compare them to lists of known phishing locations. You will be told in the toolbar if you come across a malicious site.

  • Use firewalls- it is a network security system that monitors and controls the incoming and outgoing network traffic
    based on predetermined security rules. A firewall typically establishes a barrier between a
    trusted internal network and an untrusted external network.



Install antivirus/ anti-malware protection


Antivirus software helps protect your computer from malware and cybercriminals.
Antivirus scans the computers through the network to data — webpages, files, apps, applications.
It searches for known threats and Evaluates the behaviour of the programs.
It seeks to block or remove malware as quickly as possible.



Avoid using public wi-fi


Hackers are commonly considered to have access to computers using public Wi-Fi.
So it is better to always use a mobile hotspot, instead of using public wifi.
